Erwin Schrödinger

Groundbreaker in the Field of Subatomic Physics

  • Full Name: Erwin Rudolf Josef Alexander Schrödinger
  • Born: August 12th, 1887
  • Died: January 4th, 1961
  • Nationality: Austrian
  • Field(s): Physics

    • Brief Biography

    Erwin Shrödinger was a Austrian scientist. He is mostly known for his contributions to Subatomic Physics and for his thought experiment paradox called Shrödinger's Cat. He won the Nobel Prize for Physics with his discoveries.

    Education

    He went to University of Vienna. He was well learned in the field Physics. He used his knowledge in phyisics in various spectrums such as Subatomic and Colour/Colour Perception.

    A Few of his Notable Works

  • The Schrödinger Equation
  • Schrödinger's Cat

    • Impact

    He heavily impacted the world of Physics at the time. His equation revolutionized the study of Subatomic Particles. He has a crater on the moon and an institute at the university he attended named after him.
