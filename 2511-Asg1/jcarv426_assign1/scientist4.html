Tim Berners-lee

Inventor of the World Wide Web

  • Full Name: Tim Berners-lee
  • Born: June 8th, 1955
  • Died: n/a
  • Nationality: British
  • Field(s): Physics, Computer Sciences

    • Brief Biography

    Tim Berners-lee is considered one of the many pioneers of the Internet. He created the World Wide Web which is still used today. He worked at CERN which is European Organization for Nuclear Research and he was knighted by Queen Elizabeth II for his pioneering work.

    Education

    He went to University of Oxford and studied Physics.

    A Few of his Notable Works

  • Weaving the Web
  • Co-Director of the Web Science Trust
  • Founder of the World Wide Web Foundation

    • Impact

    He invented the world wide web. It is what we use to communicate with anyone and everyone from around the globe. The majority of the 2511 class carries a computer in their hands, pockets, and/or bags. Without Sir Tim Berners-lee we wouldnt be in this class.
